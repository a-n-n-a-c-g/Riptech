Fishing

January 23, 2018

The first time I went fishing,
I pulled a crab out of the water
And held it close to my face.
Pinchers and a line,
Two beads for eyes.
I couldn’t find where
The hook stuck inside it.
Soon the crab dropped to
The ground, crawled
Over the dirt and shrubs
To disappear in the
River.

Whatever It's Called

November 5, 2017

I don’t get what the big deal is
Ya know, it’s just love,
A giant abstraction like
Freedom with its stars
Falling from the sky
And stripes like candy,
Like holidays,
Like all the things that
Settle right on the tongue.
He said it, and I didn’t say it
Back and now he stares at
Me over our stacks of textbooks
And i keep my eyes on the
Page, an object, tangible,
Not an abstraction.
Love and freedom are the same.

They Just Do

October 10, 2017

Mothers will die.
It’s a given, a surety,
A stamp on the envelope
Sent to you with the address
Of life on this great big planet
And the return address of the non-
Existent.
It is one of the big differences between
Russians and Americans. You see
They experience tragedy in the
Later years, among the aged,
Not when they’re five, ten,
Student at twenty-one.

I've Got Secrets

August 17, 2017

Secret number one:
I write poetry, but most think I’m just a computer nerd with
The capacity to write at a zero or a negative five.
Secret number two:
I’m not really Russian. Or am I? I’m American. Just kidding.
Did you really believe that?
Secret number three:
I’m not funny, but I often try to be. Sometimes I pretend so
Hard that my dog laughs, the walls break into grins.
Secret number four:
I died when I was four when a car hit me and the snow
Looked like cotton, the sky a giant pillow.
Secret number five:
I didn’t see a light when I died. I saw a man who put
His hands around my throat and said swallow, come
On i know you can do it, I know you can try.
I couldn’t.

I once saw...

June 1, 2017

A child begging for food.
You haven’t been among the really
Poor until you’ve seen a half-person,
A half-life begging to fill their belly.
My father cry when he thought
We were all asleep. He sat at the table
In the kitchen and tipped his head
Down and wept without shame. I
Didn’t know that Mom had left and
It was just him and four kids and a
Fired notice from work.
So much snow piling up
Outside like cotton or tears
Or dough, or dumplings--all the things
We would soon stand on the
Streets and beg for.

Promise

April 12, 2017

Now that you think I’m a dreadfully
depressed person,
I’m here to tell you that I’m not.
Promise.

I can tell a joke. What did the
janitor say when he came out
of the closet? Supplies!
You laughed.
Don’t lie.

I wear mismatched socks
everyday, socks which have
stripes and polka dots.
I’m eclectic and cool.
See.

I’ve been in love and out of
love and swam in the ocean
and crossed the border to
Mexico and made lots of mistakes,
Done a few things right.
It’s true.
